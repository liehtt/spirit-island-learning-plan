# PROJECT_CONTEXT: Spirit Island Mastery Roadmap

## Project Overview & Objective

The long-term objective is to fully experience and master the digital version of *Spirit Island*, incorporating all currently owned/enabled DLC expansions (Branch & Claw, Jagged Earth, Feather & Flame), exploring all available spirits, adversary families, scenarios, aspects, token systems, and eventually transitioning into optimized **2-Handed Solo** play.

The roadmap prioritizes **full content fluency and enjoyment before difficulty chasing**. Difficulty increases are a tool, not the main objective: raise difficulty only when the current stage feels too easy, decisions feel automatic, or content breadth is already stable. Very high difficulty belongs at the end as an optional mastery pressure test.

The progression framework uses isolated variables and a strict success threshold (**two consecutive victories**) to ensure progression is driven by tactical agency rather than RNG deck orders.

### Future Stage Inference

After the active Keeper gate:

1. **Phase 5 — 2-Handed Foundations:** Use 2 spirits / 2 boards, no adversary or Brandenburg-Prussia L1. Start with mastered low-complexity pairs. Advance after 2 consecutive wins and intentional cross-spirit interaction in each game.
2. **Phase 6 — Pair Library:** Build a small stable pool of 2-handed archetypes: tempo + defense, control + fear, Dahan + control, token pair. Use BP1 to BP2 only after hand management feels routine.
3. **Phase 7 — All-Spirit Experience Tour:** Give every current owned spirit a first-contact game, a competency game, and a journal profile covering win path, elements, growth pattern, reclaim pressure, pairing needs, and enjoyment.
4. **Phase 8 — Adversary Breadth:** Use known spirits against France, Habsburg Livestock, Russia, Scotland, and England to learn adversary personalities one at a time.
5. **Phase 9 — Scenario and Aspect Tour:** Add scenarios and aspects only with familiar spirits and lowered difficulty so optional content is experienced without contaminating the main learning signal.
6. **Phase 10 — Completion & Taste Cycles:** Fill gaps in spirits, adversaries, scenarios, aspects, and DLC mechanics before chasing high difficulty. Track favorites, least-favorites worth revisiting, preferred pairs, and mechanics that feel especially satisfying.
7. **Optional Challenge Endgame:** Difficulty 8–10+, extreme adversary/scenario combinations, weekly challenges, and awkward self-imposed pairings. Use this only after broad content fluency is established or when the current stage feels too easy.

### Objective Priority Order

1. Fully experience current Digital Spirit Island content and DLC systems.
2. Give every available spirit a meaningful play experience and journal profile.
3. Build comfortable 2-handed solo competence and preferred pairings.
4. Sample every adversary family, scenario family, and representative aspects.
5. Raise difficulty only as needed to keep decisions interesting.
6. Treat very high difficulty as optional endgame, not the primary path.
